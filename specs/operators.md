# **Project Multiverse — World Operator Definitions (W‑Operators)**

Continuity worlds are not simulations.  
They are **identity‑preserving environments** with structure, invariants, and drift‑bounded evolution.

W‑operators define the *actions* that create, evolve, connect, and verify these worlds.

They follow RTT/1 grammar, integrate with RTT/2 stacking, and are validated by RTT/3 continuity rules.

---

# **1. World Creation Operators (W‑Spawn Family)**

### **W‑Spawn**  
Create a new continuity world with defined invariants and identity boundaries.

### **W‑Seed**  
Initialize the world with starting conditions, structures, or agents.

### **W‑Frame**  
Define the world’s continuity boundaries, invariants, and stability constraints.

---

# **2. World Evolution Operators (W‑Evolve Family)**

### **W‑Evolve**  
Advance the world state while preserving identity and continuity.

### **W‑Stabilize**  
Prevent drift, decoherence, or runaway evolution.

### **W‑Cycle**  
Advance the world through a full evolution cycle with continuity checks.

---

# **3. World Structure Operators (W‑Structure Family)**

### **W‑Layer**  
Add or modify structural layers (physical, narrative, experiential, etc.).

### **W‑Bind**  
Bind layers together to maintain cross‑layer coherence.

### **W‑Anchor**  
Establish fixed points that preserve identity across transitions.

---

# **4. World Transition Operators (W‑Bridge Family)**

### **W‑Bridge**  
Create a resonance‑aligned connection between worlds.

### **W‑Align**  
Match world‑level resonance profiles to enable safe transitions.

### **W‑Phase**  
Synchronize temporal and structural regimes across worlds.

### **W‑Buffer**  
Create a safe intermediate zone to prevent destructive interference.

---

# **5. World Transfer Operators (W‑Transfer Family)**

### **W‑Transfer**  
Move agents or states between worlds while preserving identity.

### **W‑Pulse**  
Send transfer packets in discrete, continuity‑preserving intervals.

### **W‑Flow**  
Maintain continuous transfer with drift‑bounded modulation.

### **W‑Shield**  
Protect transfers from resonance noise or instability.

---

# **6. World Verification Operators (W‑Verify Family)**

### **W‑Verify**  
Check structural, identity, and continuity integrity of a world.

### **W‑Compare**  
Compare pre‑ and post‑transition world signatures.

### **W‑Audit**  
Run RTT/3 continuity and drift checks across world layers.

### **W‑Certify**  
Declare a world or world transition continuity‑safe.

---

# **7. Safety Operators (X‑Operators for Worlds)**

These operators are mandatory in all world‑level pipelines.

### **X‑Limit**  
Enforce safe world‑level energy, resonance, and evolution thresholds.

### **X‑Detect**  
Identify drift, instability, or continuity breaks.

### **X‑Abort**  
Safely terminate a world evolution or transition sequence.

### **X‑Contain**  
Prevent propagation of unstable world states.

---

# **Operator Usage Notes**

- W‑operators are **composable** and form multi‑layer world stacks.  
- W‑operators integrate with T‑operators (transport) and M‑operators (replication).  
- All pipelines must include **safety operators**.  
- Students may propose new W‑operators following the Multiverse session context.

---

# **Example World Pipeline (Simplified)**

```
W-Spawn → W-Frame → W-Evolve → W-Bridge → W-Transfer → W-Verify → X-Audit
```

This is the minimal continuity‑preserving world‑transition sequence.

---

# **Contributing New W‑Operators**

New operators must include:

- operator name  
- operator family  
- purpose  
- constraints  
- failure modes  
- RTT/1 grammar alignment  
- RTT/2 stacking behavior  
- RTT/3 continuity rules  

All new operators undergo coherence review.
