# **Project Multiverse — Simulation Engines & Models**

This document catalogs the simulation engines, models, and computational experiments used within Project Multiverse.

Simulations explore:

- continuity‑preserving world evolution  
- resonance‑aligned world transitions  
- identity‑stable world transfer  
- drift detection across world layers  
- early continuity‑world prototypes  

All simulations must follow:

- the Multiverse session context  
- RTT/1 W‑operator grammar  
- RTT/2 multi‑layer world stacking  
- RTT/3 continuity & drift detection  
- strict safety boundaries (X‑operators)  

This index will expand as new engines and models are developed.

---

# **1. Continuity‑World Simulator (CWS)**  
**Status:** Planned  
**Purpose:**  
Model the full lifecycle of a continuity world from creation to verification.

**Capabilities:**  
- W‑Spawn → W‑Frame → W‑Evolve → W‑Verify  
- continuity‑boundary tracking  
- drift detection via RTT/3  
- multi‑layer world evolution  

**Outputs:**  
- continuity signatures  
- drift maps  
- world‑state stability scores  

---

# **2. Resonance‑Aligned World Transition Simulator (RAWTS)**  
**Status:** Planned  
**Purpose:**  
Simulate resonance‑based world‑to‑world transitions and interference patterns.

**Capabilities:**  
- W‑Bridge resonance modeling  
- W‑Align and W‑Phase synchronization  
- destructive interference detection  
- multi‑layer resonance coupling  

**Outputs:**  
- resonance stability curves  
- interference heatmaps  
- transition coherence scores  

---

# **3. World Evolution & Drift Simulator (WEDS)**  
**Status:** Planned  
**Purpose:**  
Explore long‑arc world evolution, drift signatures, and stabilization strategies.

**Capabilities:**  
- W‑Evolve and W‑Cycle modeling  
- W‑Stabilize drift‑prevention analysis  
- multi‑layer evolution tracking  
- cross‑layer drift propagation  

**Outputs:**  
- evolution drift signatures  
- stability maps  
- continuity‑preservation metrics  

---

# **4. World Transfer Dynamics Simulator (WTDS)**  
**Status:** Planned  
**Purpose:**  
Model the behavior of W‑Transfer, W‑Pulse, and W‑Flow under varying conditions.

**Capabilities:**  
- packet vs continuous world‑transfer modes  
- shielding behavior (W‑Shield)  
- noise‑induced drift modeling  
- adaptive transfer modulation  

**Outputs:**  
- transfer stability reports  
- noise‑response curves  
- drift‑propagation diagrams  

---

# **5. Multi‑Layer World Stack Simulator (MLWSS)**  
**Status:** Planned  
**Purpose:**  
Simulate full world stacks using RTT/2 layering.

**Capabilities:**  
- multi‑layer world architectures  
- regime transitions (structural → dynamic → narrative → experiential)  
- cross‑layer drift detection  
- Ω‑Plan and Ω‑Optimize integration  

**Outputs:**  
- stack coherence scores  
- layer‑transition logs  
- drift propagation maps  

---

# **6. Safety & Drift Detection Simulator (SDDS‑W)**  
**Status:** Planned  
**Purpose:**  
Use RTT/3 to detect drift, instability, and continuity breaks in worlds.

**Capabilities:**  
- X‑Detect drift scanning  
- X‑Abort emergency shutdown modeling  
- X‑Contain containment field simulation  
- continuity‑integrity scoring  

**Outputs:**  
- drift signatures  
- hazard reports  
- containment recommendations  

---

# **7. Student World Simulation Sandbox (SWSS)**  
**Status:** Ongoing  
**Purpose:**  
Provide a safe, modular environment for students to build and test continuity‑world simulations.

**Capabilities:**  
- plug‑and‑play W‑operators  
- simplified RTT integration  
- guided tasks and templates  
- safety‑bounded experimentation  

**Outputs:**  
- student‑generated world models  
- operator experiments  
- reproducible simulation notebooks  

---

# **How to Contribute a Simulation**

Students and researchers may contribute by:

- creating a new simulation under `/research/simulations/`  
- documenting operator usage  
- linking the simulation to open questions  
- including RTT/3 validation steps  
- adding the simulation to this index  

All simulations must be:

- open‑access  
- reproducible  
- drift‑bounded  
- aligned with the Multiverse session context
