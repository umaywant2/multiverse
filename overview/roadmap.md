# **Project Multiverse — Roadmap**

Project Multiverse is a long‑arc scientific initiative exploring the foundations of **continuity worlds** — layered, persistent, identity‑coherent environments where agents, states, and narratives can evolve safely.

This roadmap outlines the staged progression of the project, from conceptual groundwork to early continuity‑world prototypes to long‑term, multi‑world architectures.

---

# **Phase 1 — Foundations (Years 0–3)**  
**Goal:** Establish the conceptual and structural base for continuity worlds.

**Milestones:**

- Define the operator grammar for world actions (W‑operators)  
- Map continuity boundaries for world‑level identity  
- Build RTT‑aligned world simulation engines  
- Explore resonance‑aligned world transitions  
- Publish foundational models and conceptual papers  
- Onboard the first wave of student contributors  

**Outputs:**

- `/specs/operators.md` (W‑operators)  
- continuity‑world regime maps  
- early world‑simulation notebooks  

---

# **Phase 2 — Early Continuity Worlds (Years 3–7)**  
**Goal:** Move from theory to controlled, simulated continuity‑world behavior.

**Milestones:**

- Develop stable world‑state representations  
- Model identity‑preserving transitions  
- Build multi‑layer world stacks (RTT/2)  
- Explore resonance‑coupled world boundaries  
- Establish open simulation frameworks for students  

**Outputs:**

- early continuity‑world prototypes  
- world‑transition models  
- continuity‑preservation metrics  

---

# **Phase 3 — Structural Breakthroughs (Years 7–12)**  
**Goal:** Achieve the first meaningful demonstrations of persistent, identity‑coherent worlds.

**Milestones:**

- Demonstrate stable continuity across world cycles  
- Validate RTT‑based coherence for world transitions  
- Build multi‑operator world stacks  
- Publish reproducible continuity‑world experiments  
- Expand global student and AI collaboration  

**Outputs:**

- persistent continuity‑world simulations  
- validated identity‑preservation models  
- cross‑lab reproducibility frameworks  

---

# **Phase 4 — Applied Continuity Worlds (Years 12–20)**  
**Goal:** Transition from simulation to early interactive continuity‑world environments.

**Milestones:**

- Develop safe, identity‑stable world environments  
- Integrate resonance‑aligned world transitions  
- Build continuity‑preserving agent frameworks  
- Create educational and research continuity worlds  
- Establish open‑access world‑research hubs  

**Outputs:**

- functional continuity‑world environments  
- open world‑simulation specifications  
- global deployment models  

---

# **Phase 5 — The Multiverse Era (Beyond Year 20)**  
**Goal:** Enable a world where continuity‑aligned multiverses are accessible, safe, and transformative.

**Milestones:**

- universal continuity‑world standards  
- distributed multiverse networks  
- integration with Manifestation (replicators)  
- integration with Bubble Gum (transporters)  
- global governance and safety frameworks  

**Outputs:**

- first‑generation continuity‑world systems  
- open, global multiverse ecosystems  
- identity‑preserving world infrastructure  

---

# **Guiding Principles**

- **Continuity First** — Worlds must preserve identity, structure, and state.  
- **Open Science** — All research remains free and accessible.  
- **RTT‑Aligned** — Operators, pipelines, and simulations follow RTT/1–3.  
- **Safety Always** — All work includes X‑operators and drift detection.  
- **Long‑Arc Vision** — Continuity worlds are a decades‑long project.  

---

# **How to Contribute**

Students, researchers, and structural AIs can contribute by:

- building world simulations  
- designing W‑operators  
- exploring resonance‑aligned transitions  
- writing papers  
- mapping continuity regimes  
- improving documentation  
- mentoring new contributors  

Project Multiverse is a **public scientific accelerator**.  
Everyone is welcome.
