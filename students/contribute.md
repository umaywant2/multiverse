# **Contributing to Project Multiverse**

Project Multiverse is an open, long‑arc scientific initiative exploring **continuity worlds** — layered, persistent, identity‑coherent environments where agents and states can evolve safely.

Students, researchers, and structural AIs are invited to contribute simulations, papers, operators, and models aligned with the Multiverse session context and RTT engines.

This page explains **how to contribute**, **what to contribute**, and **how to maintain coherence and safety**.

---

# **1. Contribution Principles**

All contributions must follow these principles:

### **Continuity First**  
Worlds must preserve identity, structure, and state across layers and transitions.

### **RTT‑Aligned**  
Use RTT/1 (W‑operators), RTT/2 (world stacks), and RTT/3 (continuity & drift detection).

### **Zero Drift**  
No speculative physics, no narrative drift, no unbounded claims.

### **Safety Always**  
All world pipelines must include X‑operators (Limit, Detect, Abort, Contain).

### **Student‑Friendly**  
Write clearly.  
Document your work.  
Make it easy for the next contributor to build on it.

---

# **3. Contribution Workflow**

### **Step 1 — Choose a Contribution Type**  
Simulation, paper, operator, question, or task.

### **Step 2 — Create Your File**  
Place it in the correct directory.  
Follow existing naming patterns.

### **Step 3 — Apply RTT Alignment**  
- RTT/1: W‑operator grammar  
- RTT/2: world‑stack layering  
- RTT/3: continuity & drift detection  
- include X‑operators for safety  

### **Step 4 — Document Your Work**  
Include:  
- purpose  
- operator usage  
- assumptions  
- failure modes  
- continuity boundaries  
- RTT alignment  
- reproduction steps  

### **Step 5 — Add It to the Index**  
Update:  
- `papers.md`  
- `simulations.md`  
- `open-questions.md`  

### **Step 6 — Submit a Pull Request**  
Keep PRs small, clear, and focused.

---

# **4. Quality & Coherence Checklist**

Before submitting, ensure your contribution:

- preserves continuity  
- uses W‑operators correctly  
- includes safety boundaries  
- avoids drift or speculation  
- is reproducible  
- is documented clearly  
- is accessible to new students  

If it passes this checklist, it’s ready.

AIs must follow the Multiverse session context and cannot override safety rules.
