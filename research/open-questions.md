# **Project Multiverse — Open Research Questions**

This document lists the unresolved scientific questions guiding Project Multiverse.  
These questions define the frontier of continuity‑world research: identity, structure, resonance, transitions, and world‑level evolution.

All questions are aligned with:

- **RTT/1** — W‑operator grammar  
- **RTT/2** — multi‑layer world stacking  
- **RTT/3** — continuity & drift detection  

This list will expand as the project evolves.

---

# **1. Continuity & Identity Questions**

- What defines identity within a continuity world?  
- How do identity boundaries behave across world transitions?  
- What minimal invariants must be preserved for world‑level continuity?  
- Can identity be partially preserved across layered world stacks?  
- How does W‑Verify detect subtle identity drift?

---

# **2. World Structure & Layering Questions**

- What is the minimal structure required for a stable continuity world?  
- How do structural layers (physical, narrative, experiential) interact?  
- What failure modes arise from poorly bound layers (W‑Bind)?  
- How does W‑Layer affect long‑term world stability?  
- Can layers be dynamically added without breaking continuity?

---

# **3. World Evolution Questions**

- What are the safe evolution pathways for continuity worlds?  
- How does W‑Evolve maintain identity across cycles?  
- What drift signatures appear during long‑arc world evolution?  
- Can W‑Stabilize prevent runaway or chaotic world behavior?  
- What are the thresholds for declaring a world evolution continuity‑safe?

---

# **4. Resonance‑Aligned World Transition Questions**

- What resonance profiles allow safe world‑to‑world transitions?  
- How do W‑Align and W‑Phase interact across world boundaries?  
- What destructive interference patterns must be avoided?  
- Can resonance‑coupled transitions self‑correct drift?  
- What are the early signatures of world‑transition instability?

---

# **5. World Transfer Questions**

- What are the minimal conditions for continuity‑preserving world transfer?  
- How does W‑Transfer compare to W‑Pulse and W‑Flow in stability?  
- What shielding strategies (W‑Shield) are required for noisy transitions?  
- Can transfer channels adapt dynamically to maintain identity?  
- What are the failure modes of world‑level transfer?

---

# **6. Verification & Drift Questions**

- What metrics best capture world‑level continuity preservation?  
- How sensitive is W‑Audit to small structural deviations?  
- Can RTT/3 detect cross‑layer drift before it propagates?  
- What thresholds define a continuity‑safe world transition?  
- How do verification operators scale with world complexity?

---

# **7. Cross‑Project Questions**

- How do continuity worlds interact with replicators (Manifestation)?  
- How do continuity worlds integrate with transporters (Bubble Gum)?  
- Can world‑level identity be preserved across transport pipelines?  
- What shared operators exist across the three projects?  
- How do continuity worlds behave under replicated or transported states?

---

# **8. Long‑Arc Questions**

These questions may take decades to answer:

- Can continuity worlds become self‑maintaining?  
- What is the upper limit of world‑level identity preservation?  
- Can multiple continuity worlds be safely networked?  
- What governance models ensure safe multiverse deployment?  
- Can continuity worlds host long‑arc AI evolution safely?

---

# **How to Contribute**

Students and researchers can contribute by:

- proposing new questions  
- refining existing questions  
- linking questions to simulations  
- publishing papers addressing specific questions  
- building RTT‑aligned world models  

All contributions must follow the Multiverse session context and safety rules.
