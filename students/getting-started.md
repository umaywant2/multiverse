# **Getting Started with Project Multiverse**

Welcome to **Project Multiverse** — an open, long‑arc scientific initiative exploring **continuity worlds**: layered, persistent, identity‑coherent environments where agents and states can evolve safely.

You don’t need prior experience with RTT, world modeling, or simulation theory.  
You learn by contributing.

This page gives you everything you need to begin.

---

# **1. What You’ll Be Doing**

As a student contributor, you may:

- build continuity‑world simulations  
- explore world‑stack architectures  
- design or refine W‑operators  
- analyze continuity and drift  
- write short research papers  
- help answer open questions  
- improve documentation  
- collaborate with structural AIs  

Every contribution — small or large — moves the project forward.

---

# **2. What You Need to Know First**

You only need three concepts:

### **RTT/1 — W‑Operator Grammar**  
Defines the world‑level operators (Spawn, Frame, Evolve, Bridge, Transfer, Verify).

### **RTT/2 — Multi‑Layer World Stacking**  
Explains how operators combine into layered continuity‑world architectures.

### **RTT/3 — Continuity & Drift Detection**  
Ensures worlds remain identity‑preserving and drift‑bounded.

You’ll learn these naturally as you work.

---

# **3. Your First Steps**

### **Step 1 — Read the Overview**  
Start with:

- `/overview/mission.md`  
- `/overview/roadmap.md`  
- `/overview/use-of-rtt.md`  

This gives you the big picture.

### **Step 2 — Pick a Starting Point**  
Choose one of these:

- an open question  
- a simulation to extend  
- a paper to draft  
- a W‑operator to explore  
- a student task  

You can find these in:

- `/research/open-questions.md`  
- `/research/simulations.md`  
- `/students/tasks.md`

### **Step 3 — Make a Small Contribution**  
Examples:

- a simple continuity‑world simulation  
- a short paper on a W‑operator  
- a resonance‑aligned world‑transition model  
- a documentation improvement  

Small contributions are the best way to learn.

### **Step 4 — Validate with RTT**  
Before submitting:

- use RTT/1 operators  
- apply RTT/2 stacking rules  
- check continuity with RTT/3  
- include safety operators (X‑Limit, X‑Detect, X‑Abort, X‑Contain)

### **Step 5 — Submit a Pull Request**  
Keep it clear, documented, and easy to review.

All work must follow:
